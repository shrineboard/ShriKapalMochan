Shri Kapal Mochan

Kapal Mochan is an ancient place of pilgrimage for both Hindu and Sikhs, 17 km north-east of Jagadhari town, on the Bilaspur road in Yamuna nagar district. It is also called Gopal Mochan and Somsar Mochan. As per Legend, Brāhmanahatya i.e. killing of Brahmin is considered as a major sin, but one who kills a Brahmin and bath here, his Brāhmanahatya sins will be washed.

Nearby Bilaspur, Haryana (not to be confused with Bilaspur, Himachal Pradesh) in Yamuna Nagar District which takes its name from the corrupted form of "Vyas Puri", was the ashram of Ved Vyasa rishi where he wrote the Mahabharata on the banks of Sarasvati river near Adi Badri where Sarasvati river leaves Himalayas and enters the plains. It is one of the most ancient Vedic religious site in Haryana along with 48 kos parikrama of Kurukshetra and Dhosi Hill.

Shri Badri Narayan

This place is considered to be the ancient abode of Lord Shri Adibadri Narayan. According to the legends, Lord Vishnu resided at this place in all the three eras of Satyug, Tretayug, and Dwaparayuga and even after the arrival of Kaliyug, at present, the famous pilgrimage site of Uttaranchal went to Badri Dham, before Lord Shri Vishnu had come to Adibadri Dham. Therefore this place has the distinction of being the oldest.

Shri Mantra Devi

Mantra Devi Temple is Yamuna nagar in Haryana is dedicated to Goddess Lakshmi and is located a top the Shivalik Mountain. To reach the temple one has reach Jagadhri, from there take the road to Bilaspur and then by passing through Ranjeetpur and Kathgarh Village to reach the mountain.

Shri Kedar Nath

This is another ancient temple just about 200 meter from adi badri temple. The Shivling here is a Swayambhu (Self Manifested) and was enshrined by Adi Shanakaracharya. Many rishises and munies have meditated here over the centuries gone by.

Shri Kapal Mochan

Guru Gobind Singh visited Kapal Mochan after Battle of Bhangani in 1688 and gave robes of honour (turbans) to soldiers who fought this victorious war against Hill Rulers. He also had discourse with priests of temple on Durga. He gave Hukamnama to temple a priest which is still preserved by them. Also, Guru Gobind Singh and his soldiers get temple, rid of those people, who pollute the pond water, by doing toilets on short distances from ponds.[11] In Dasam Granth, Khalsa Mahima (the praise of Khalsa) and Charitar 71 describe a few events happened during Gobind Singh's stay at Kapal Mochan.

Saraswati Udgam Sthal

Sri Sarasvati Udgam Tirath, is a tourist site of archaeological, religious and ecological significance in a forest area in the foothills of the Sivalik Hills in Bhabar area, situated in northern part of Yamunanagar district, of the north Indian state of Haryana. There are remains of many Buddhist stupas and monasteries, which are about 1500–2000 years old, and there is also a group of Hindu temples from the 9th century.

Martial Art Museum

The Guru Gobind Singh Martial Art Museum is situated near the famous pilgrimage Kapal Mochan, Yamunanagar. Kapal Mochan is considered to be holy as it is believed that Guru Nanak Dev and Guru Gobind Singh had visited the place.. The inscription can be assigned to 7 th -8 th century A.D. On August 15, 2000 the foundation stone of Guru Gobind Singh Martial Art Museum at Kapal Mochan, Yamunanagar was placed. The district administration had handed over the museum to the State Archaeology Department in June 2011..

Rin Mochan

It is believed that on the day of Kartik Purnima, Lord Shiva was freed from Brahma Dosha by bathing in the lake situated at Kapal Mochan. For this reason, every year a 5-day fair is held here on Kartik Purnima. In which lakhs of people take bath in the lake to get rid of sins. Kapal Mochan, Loan Mochan and Surya Kund are three lakes located here.

Suraj Kund Sarowar

There will be a unique confluence of faith in Kapalmochan on November 15. It is said that by bathing in the Soma (Surajkund) lake located at Kapal Mochan, Lord Shiva was freed from Brahma Dosha. For this reason, a fair is held here every year on Kartik Purnima, in which lakhs of people come to bathe in the lakes to get rid of sins, hence it is famous by the name of Kapalmochan Mela.

Gau Bacha Ghat

On one side of Kapalmochan Sarovar there is a black Gau-Bachha and on one end there is a white Gau-Bachha Ghat. When he took a bath in Kapalmochan Sarovar on Kartik Purnima, his complexion turned white and he was freed from the sin of killing. Guru Gobind Singh and Guru Nanak Dev Ji also came here on Kartik Purnima. Since then the Sikhs started celebrating the birthday of Guru Nanak Dev Ji here. After this a fair started here on Kartik Purnima..
